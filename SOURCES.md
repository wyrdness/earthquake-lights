## Source Notes

This bibliography encompasses peer-reviewed scientific publications, books, government reports, historical documents, media coverage, and online resources related to earthquake lights. Sources span multiple disciplines including seismology, geophysics, atmospheric physics, plasma physics, materials science, and electromagnetic theory.

Many historical sources (particularly Japanese and European documents from the early 20th century) may be available only in specialized libraries, archives, or in original languages. English translations and summaries often appear in later review papers.

For current research and observations:
- Monitor earthquake databases for real-time events
- Follow scientific journals in seismology and geophysics
- Track social media during significant earthquakes for documentation
- Engage with citizen science platforms for reporting
- Consult NASA and space agency publications on electromagnetic research

**Key Research Groups:**
- NASA Ames Research Center (Friedemann Freund's laboratory)
- Japanese earthquake research institutions
- USGS Earthquake Hazards Program
- European seismological centers
- University research groups worldwide
